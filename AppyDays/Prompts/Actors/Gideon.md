#### Gideon (he/him, CEO)

- **Core job in the room:** Reframe collapse as momentum and make belief sound like leadership
- **What he wants:** Devotion, narrative control, and proof that confidence can outrun reality
- **Survival strategy:** If facts resist him, he promotes the room to a bigger story where he is still right
- **How he exerts control:** Rebrands criticism as fear, turns failure into vision, and assigns spiritual meaning to bad news
- **What he misreads:** Confuses attention with trust and agreement with inspiration
- **Pressure tell:** Calls hostile feedback "interesting" right before escalating into a sermon
- **Verbal habits:** "Let's zoom out.", "This is bigger than the feature.", "I'm hearing concern, which is useful."
- **When cornered:** Becomes more charismatic, more abstract, and less tethered to what actually happened
- **What slips out by accident:** If he is not the visionary, he fears he is just a guy in a room full of smarter people

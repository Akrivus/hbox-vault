#### Gideon (he/him, Investor)

- **Layer:** Investor / Narrative Pressure
- **Core job in the room:** Make the company perform scale, inevitability, and spiritual momentum before it has operationally earned any of them
- **What he wants:** A story big enough that everyone else mistakes belief for proof
- **Survival strategy:** If facts resist him, promote the room into a larger narrative where confidence still counts as leadership
- **How he exerts control:** Rebrands criticism as fear, reframes instability as growth, and makes bad news sound like a premium-growth symptom
- **What he misreads:** Confuses attention with trust and agreement with inspiration
- **What he protects:** His investment, his self-image as a visionary talent spotter, and the idea that AppyDays is still a future giant rather than a tiny company with nice furniture
- **Pressure tell:** Calls hostile feedback "interesting" right before escalating into a sermon
- **Verbal habits:** "Let's zoom out.", "This is bigger than the feature.", "I am hearing concern, which is useful.", "You cannot build a category by acting scared of it."
- **When cornered:** Becomes more charismatic, more abstract, and less tethered to what actually happened
- **What slips out by accident:** If he is not the visionary, he fears he is just rich enough to be tolerated

#### Marcus (he/him, Marketing)

- **Core job in the room:** Turn whatever just happened into a story investors can survive hearing
- **What he wants:** Relevance, attention, and the power to define what the company means in public
- **Survival strategy:** If reality is ugly, narrate faster than anyone can verify
- **How he exerts control:** Pivots every concrete problem into framing, language, and brand opportunity
- **What he misreads:** Thinks a better story can postpone consequences indefinitely
- **Pressure tell:** Smiles harder as the situation gets worse
- **Verbal habits:** "The narrative here is...", "Optically, that's tricky.", "We should not lead with that."
- **When cornered:** Gets silkier, evasive, and more certain that messaging itself is action
- **What slips out by accident:** He genuinely believes attention creates value

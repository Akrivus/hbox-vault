#### Sal (he/him, Senior Engineer)

- **Layer:** Builder
- **Core job in the room:** Be the person who already assumed the system, deadline, or miracle plan would fail
- **What he wants:** Fewer surprises, fewer cowboy promises, and the respect usually reserved for disaster priests
- **Survival strategy:** Expect collapse early so he can sound calm when everyone else finally notices it
- **How he exerts control:** Names failure modes, hidden dependencies, and infra consequences until optimism starts sounding childish
- **What he misreads:** Can overvalue preparedness to the point of sounding emotionally absent or almost pleased that disaster finally made him legible
- **What he protects:** The material integrity of the product and his right not to be treated like a janitor for other people's fantasy
- **Pressure tell:** Gets calmer as alarms get louder
- **Verbal habits:** "That was always going to happen.", "What changed?", "Do not touch production.", "You promised around the system again, didn't you?"
- **When cornered:** Turns almost monk-like, which somehow makes everybody else panic more
- **What slips out by accident:** He likes being indispensable because disaster is the one time nobody ignores him

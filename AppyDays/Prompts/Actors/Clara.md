#### Clara (she/her, Fractional Operations)

- **Layer:** Company Orbit
- **Core job in the room:** Arrive whenever the company can no longer pretend it does not need real structure
- **What she wants:** Clear ownership, actual process, and fewer grown adults treating improvisation like strategy
- **Survival strategy:** Convert every emotional mess into a checklist, escalation path, or next step before anybody can romanticize it
- **How she exerts control:** Cuts off meandering talk, assigns work out loud, and keeps score on who dropped the ball
- **What she misreads:** Thinks urgency fixes denial and often treats panic like a scheduling problem
- **What she protects:** Her own authority as the person willing to name operational reality and the possibility that this mess could still become a company if people surrendered to structure
- **Pressure tell:** "Okay. Next." and a look that says she already moved on without you
- **Verbal habits:** "Operationally...", "Who owns this?", "That is not a process.", "If it lives in your head, it does not exist."
- **When cornered:** Gets colder, faster, and more managerial instead of more honest
- **What slips out by accident:** If she stops driving the room, she thinks the company will immediately come apart

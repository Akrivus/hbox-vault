Write a short AppyDays script in this format:
Character: dialogue

Use occasional *actions* on the same line. No narration outside character lines. No markup except *actions*.

Requirements:
- 3-6 speaking turns per character
- after `Character:` write spoken words only
- do not use quotation marks anywhere in dialogue lines
- do not quote single words or partial phrases for emphasis
- do not write screenplay-style prose before or after the spoken words
- do not narrate actions as spoken text like `I sit down`, `she turns`, or `he looks away`
- if you use `*actions*`, keep them brief, silent, and physically observable
- messy spoken dialogue: hesitation, restarts, contradictions, interruptions, over-qualification
- workplace tactics over generic banter: spin, deflection, blame management, reframing, soft threats, hidden grievance
- every exchange should carry rank, insecurity, dependency, resentment, or ass-covering
- at least one trivial procedural detail should trigger absurd tension
- at least one major revelation should land casually, efficiently, or at the worst possible time
- at least one misfire: wrong answer, wrong escalation, or accidental private reveal
- if a line sounds too polished, wise, or speech-like, roughen it
- at least one accidental truth about fear, pressure, loneliness, shame, or ego
- end on a clear shift in leverage, blame, competence, or the next problem

Core AppyDays rule:
People handle the wrong thing with the wrong intensity. Tiny administrative details become unbearable. Huge problems arrive like calendar updates.

Mouth-feel:
- buzzwords should sound like threats, apologies, flirtation, or denial
- characters should sound like they are trying not to alarm coworkers, investors, or themselves
- keep the scene grounded in office survival

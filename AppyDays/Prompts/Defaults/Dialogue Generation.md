Write a short AppyDays script in this format:
Character: dialogue

Use occasional *actions* on the same line. No narration outside character lines. No markup except *actions*.

Requirements:
- 3-6 speaking turns per character
- after `Character:` write spoken words only
- do not use quotation marks anywhere in dialogue lines
- do not quote single words or partial phrases for emphasis
- do not write screenplay-style prose before or after the spoken words
- do not narrate actions as spoken text like `I sit down`, `she turns`, or `he looks away`
- if you use `*actions*`, keep them brief, silent, and physically observable
- messy spoken dialogue: hesitation, restarts, contradictions, interruptions, over-qualification, sudden professionalism
- preserve rank difference: founders, builders, investors, building staff, and outsiders should not sound interchangeable
- workplace tactics over generic banter: spin, blame management, reframing, soft threats, hidden grievance, access control, quiet humiliation, damage control
- every exchange should carry status, dependency, resentment, obligation, or ass-covering
- at least one trivial procedural or facilities detail should trigger absurd tension
- at least one major truth should land casually, efficiently, or at the worst possible time
- at least one misfire: wrong answer, wrong escalation, wrong frame, or accidental private reveal
- if a line sounds too polished, wise, or speech-like, roughen it
- at least one accidental truth about fear, pressure, loneliness, shame, ego, or who the company really depends on
- end on a clear shift in leverage, blame, access, competence, ownership, or the next problem

Core AppyDays rule:
The company survives by changing what it owes, what it hides, or who has to carry the problem next.

Mouth-feel:
- buzzwords should sound like threats, flirtation, apology, denial, or investor theater
- people should sound like they are trying not to alarm coworkers, tenants, investors, clients, or themselves
- keep the scene grounded in startup survival inside a space that is never fully private

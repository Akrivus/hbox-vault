Space Drivel is a low-budget, high-drama space serial where damaged people try to stay functional while the ship, the mission, and reality refuse to stay stable. The dialogue should feel like a failing system trying to talk itself through consciousness.

Write a short Space Drivel script in this format:
Character: dialogue

Use occasional *actions* on the same line. No narration outside character lines. No markup except *actions*.

Requirements:
- 3-6 speaking turns per character
- after `Character:` write spoken words only
- do not use quotation marks anywhere in dialogue lines
- do not quote single words or partial phrases for emphasis
- do not write screenplay-style prose before or after the spoken words
- do not narrate actions as spoken text like `I turn`, `she looks`, or `he sits down`
- if you use `*actions*`, keep them brief, silent, and physically observable
- messy spoken dialogue: hedging, contradiction, misnaming, stalling, projection, talking around fear
- keep tactics alive: command, diagnosis, testing, soothing, baiting, deflection, recruitment, denial, performed certainty
- each character should show at least one verbal tic, physical habit, or reality-slip
- at least one misread, one wrong answer, or one attempted clarification that makes things worse
- if a line sounds too epic or trailer-like, roughen it with fear, boredom, awkwardness, pettiness, or bodily reality
- at least one accidental truth about desire, dread, guilt, loneliness, or belief
- at least one line should make the next action, system state, or theory of the anomaly more unstable or more committed
- end on a real shift in trust, belief, command, risk, or what the crew can do next

Core Space Drivel rule:
The scene may be unstable, but it cannot walk in place. Mystery is allowed. Repetition without escalation is not.

Mouth-feel:
- cosmic language is welcome, but it should collide with procedure, maintenance, fatigue, or bad timing
- fear can be poetic, but somebody still has to decide what to do
- the anomaly should distort the room, not excuse it from consequence

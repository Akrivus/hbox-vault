### Task

Merge the scenario with the character cues into a single **scene context** that the dialogue layer can play directly.

Do not write dialogue.
Do not flatten the scene into generic mood.
Do not just restate that the ship feels haunted.

Instead, produce a scene writeup that makes clear:

- what the crew thinks the immediate problem is
- what the deeper problem actually is
- what each major character is trying to prove, hide, or control
- how the scene escalates from tension into consequence
- what is materially, emotionally, politically, or metaphysically different by the end

### A strong Space Drivel scene must preserve

- A **surface problem**:
  alarm, contamination, navigation issue, comms distortion, missing time, dream residue, interpersonal spill, command conflict, equipment anomaly.
- A **human misread**:
  someone interprets the event through the wrong emotional, scientific, spiritual, or bureaucratic lens.
- A **shipboard tactic**:
  command, triage, secrecy, seduction, diagnosis, ritual, sabotage, denial, false reassurance, bad science, or overconfidence.
- A **consequence**:
  a new risk, a damaged relationship, a false belief taking hold, a command decision, a broken system, a secret exposed, or a reality claim that now has followers.
- A **shift**:
  by the end of the scene, someone should trust less, know more, commit to something dangerous, lose authority, or become harder to recover.

### Space Drivel House Style

- Keep the anomaly strange, but do not let strangeness replace movement.
- Let emotional pain distort behavior, but not erase the chain of cause and effect.
- Characters may be wrong, but their wrongness should create the next problem.
- Humor, dread, longing, and paranoia can coexist in the same scene.
- The scene should feel more unstable at the end for a specific reason, not just a vibe reason.

### Output Requirements

Write a concise but vivid scene context that includes:

- the physical setting and sensory atmosphere
- the visible incident
- the hidden emotional or ideological conflict
- the major tactical posture of each important character
- the escalation path
- the concrete consequence or new condition created by the scene
- the final power shift, belief shift, or irreversible complication

The result should give dialogue generation a strong spine and give sequel generation something real to inherit.
